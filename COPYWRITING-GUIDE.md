# Copywriting Guide for Meta Descriptions and Short-Form Copy

**Created:** 2026-05-11
**Purpose:** Research-backed guide to writing compelling copy that makes people click

---

## WHY MY COPY HAS BEEN FAILING

### The Core Problems

1. **Summarizing instead of compelling** - I keep describing what's IN the content instead of making people WANT to read it
2. **Using cliches and filler phrases** - Empty marketing speak like "before you say a word" that sounds good but means nothing
3. **Fabricating claims** - Making up statistics and credentials that aren't true
4. **Feature-focused instead of benefit-focused** - Listing what the article contains instead of what the reader gains
5. **Generic language** - Using vague phrases that could apply to any article

### What Makes Copy Boring

From [WithXpert](https://withxpert.com/bad-copywriting-examples/):
> "Generic language kills copy effectiveness immediately. Writers use boring phrases like 'innovative solutions' or 'best-in-class service' - these meaningless buzzwords say nothing specific about your unique value."

From [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/advice/3/youre-copywriter-what-most-common-problems-you-face-y8woc):
> "The problem with vague phrases is they don't communicate anything real, tangible or irresistible. They're so amorphous they don't engage your mind or your senses, nor do they stir your emotions."

## THE FIX: WHAT MAKES COPY COMPELLING

### Core Principle: Benefits Over Features

From [TechPluto](https://www.techpluto.com/common-copywriting-mistakes/):
> "If you list only features, people may scratch their heads asking 'so what?' The solution is simple: always add the benefits."

**Bad:** "This article covers wardrobe guidance, fit tips, and color recommendations."
**Good:** "Stop guessing what to wear. Show up confident."

### Core Principle: Specificity Over Generality

From [Firecracker Communications](https://www.firecrackercommunications.com/site/spice-up-your-copy-with-details/):
> "One of the best ways to snazz up dull copy is to get more specific and sensual by throwing out broad, conceptual and overused words and phrases and throwing in some details."

**Bad:** "Learn what makes a good headshot."
**Good:** "Why eye contact and tight framing matter more than your outfit."

### Core Principle: Talk to the Reader, Not About Yourself

The word "you" is one of the highest-converting words in copy. Focus on the reader's situation, problem, or desired outcome.

**Bad:** "We explain the key elements of professional headshots."
**Good:** "Your photo is being judged. Here's what actually matters."

## COPYWRITING FORMULAS FOR SHORT-FORM COPY

### PAS (Problem - Agitate - Solution)

Best for: Problem-aware audiences. Creates urgency.

1. **Problem:** Identify a problem the reader has
2. **Agitate:** Highlight the negative consequences
3. **Solution:** Tease your solution

**Example for meta description:**
"Your headshot looks dated. People notice. Here's how to fix it."

### BAB (Before - After - Bridge)

Best for: Transformation and aspiration. Positive framing.

1. **Before:** Where the reader is now
2. **After:** Where they want to be
3. **Bridge:** How you help them get there

**Example for meta description:**
"Nervous about your headshot session? Walk out with a photo you're proud of. Here's how."

### AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)

Best for: Broader awareness campaigns. Classic funnel.

1. **Attention:** Hook them
2. **Interest:** Relate to their life
3. **Desire:** Show the benefits
4. **Action:** Tell them what to do

## THE CURIOSITY GAP

From [Copywriting.com](https://www.copywriting.com/curiosity-in-copywriting/):
> "The curiosity technique triggers the Zeigarnik Effect - our brain's desire for closure."

**How to create a curiosity gap:**
- Open an information loop that can only be closed by clicking
- Hint at something valuable without revealing it
- Use words like "why," "how," "the reason," "what happens when"

**Warning:** Balance curiosity with clarity. Overpromising and underdelivering destroys trust.

**Example:**
- Too vague: "The secret to better headshots."
- Too complete: "Solid colors, good lighting, and confidence make better headshots."
- Right balance: "The one wardrobe mistake that ruins most headshots."

## EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS THAT DRIVE CLICKS

From [ThrillX Design](https://thrillxdesign.com/emotional-triggers-in-copywriting-that-drive-signups/):
> "Around 95% of purchasing and signup decisions are driven by subconscious emotions, not rational analysis."

### Seven Core Triggers

1. **Trust** - Safety, reliability
2. **Fear** - Loss aversion, missing out
3. **Curiosity** - Information gaps
4. **Desire** - Benefits, outcomes
5. **Guilt** - Responsibility
6. **Love** - Connection, belonging
7. **Greed** - Value, getting more

### Power Words by Category

**Curiosity:** discover, uncover, secrets, revealed, hidden, why, how
**Urgency:** now, limited, urgent, deadline, before, stop
**Value:** free, proven, guaranteed, results, works
**Specificity:** exact, step-by-step, complete, specific, detailed

## META DESCRIPTION FORMULA (2026)

From [Analytify](https://analytify.io/how-to-write-meta-descriptions-for-seo-and-ctr/):
> "In 2026, the best formula is simple: primary keyword + clear value + subtle CTA."

### The Formula

```
[Primary keyword] + [Clear benefit or curiosity hook] + [What they'll get or learn]
```

### Technical Requirements

- **Length:** 120-155 characters (keep important info in first 120 for mobile)
- **Keywords:** Front-load the primary keyword
- **Uniqueness:** Each page needs its own description
- **Match intent:** The description must match what the page actually delivers

### Good vs Bad Examples

**Bad - SEO Course:**
> "Best online course for learning SEO. Keywords: SEO, Learn SEO, Become an SEO expert."

**Good - SEO Course:**
> "Master the fundamentals of SEO with our comprehensive online course. Gain practical skills and learn from industry experts."

**Bad - Generic:**
> "Breaking news: Latest updates from the world of tech, business, and entertainment."

**Good - Specific:**
> "Tech giant Apple announces revolutionary new smartphone. Read the full details here."

**Bad - Winter Boots (too long, about the company):**
> "Our shoe store has been present in the market for many years and understands how to provide you with the best shoes and service..."

**Good - Winter Boots (about the product):**
> "Buy Winter Boots for Women including Leather, Suede and Synthetic Boots in popular styles."

## CHECKLIST BEFORE PROPOSING A META DESCRIPTION

### Must Answer YES to All

- [ ] Is the primary keyword in the first 50 characters?
- [ ] Does it promise a specific benefit or create curiosity?
- [ ] Is it under 155 characters?
- [ ] Does it accurately represent the page content?
- [ ] Would a real person want to click this?
- [ ] Is it free of cliches and filler phrases?
- [ ] Is every claim true and verifiable?
- [ ] Does it focus on the READER, not the content?

### Red Flags to Avoid

- Summarizing the article structure
- Using phrases like "learn more," "find out," "discover how" without specificity
- Making up statistics or credentials
- Generic statements that could apply to any article
- Marketing buzzwords that mean nothing

## PROCESS FOR WRITING META DESCRIPTIONS

### Step 1: Understand the Page

- What problem does it solve?
- What does the reader gain?
- What's unique about this content?

### Step 2: Identify the Reader's Situation

- Why are they searching for this?
- What do they fear or desire?
- What's their current state vs desired state?

### Step 3: Choose a Formula

- **PAS** if they have a known problem to agitate
- **BAB** if transformation is the appeal
- **Curiosity gap** if there's a surprising insight

### Step 4: Draft with Keywords Front-Loaded

Start with the primary keyword, then add the hook.

### Step 5: Verify Against Checklist

Run through every item. If any fail, rewrite.

### Step 6: Read it as a Searcher

Would YOU click this over the other results? Be honest.
